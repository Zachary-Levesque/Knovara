# Market Research

## Research Goal

Determine whether technical onboarding and organizational knowledge transfer represent a large and valuable problem for engineering organizations.

---

# Problem Validation

Organizations invest significant resources in hiring engineers, yet new employees often require weeks or months before becoming fully productive.

During this ramp-up period:

* New hires spend time searching for information
* Senior employees spend time answering repetitive questions
* Projects progress more slowly
* Knowledge remains siloed

The cost of onboarding extends beyond training. It directly impacts engineering productivity.

---

# Existing Pain Points

Engineering organizations frequently struggle with:

## Fragmented Knowledge

Information is spread across:

* GitHub
* Jira
* Confluence
* Notion
* Slack
* Teams
* Internal wikis
* Documentation
* Meetings

There is often no single source of truth.

---

## Tribal Knowledge

Critical information exists only in the minds of experienced employees.

Examples:

* Why a system was built
* Historical decisions
* Known limitations
* Team-specific processes

This knowledge is difficult to discover.

---

## Slow Ramp-Up

New engineers often ask:

* Where do I start?
* What should I learn first?
* Who owns this system?
* Which documentation matters?

Finding answers can take significant time.

---

# Market Trends

Several trends increase the importance of this problem:

## Growth of Remote Work

Distributed teams rely heavily on digital knowledge.

New employees have fewer opportunities to learn informally.

---

## Increasing System Complexity

Modern software systems contain:

* Multiple repositories
* Microservices
* Cloud infrastructure
* Large documentation sets

Understanding these systems is increasingly difficult.

---

## Increased Hiring

Growing companies continuously onboard:

* Interns
* New graduates
* Experienced hires
* Contractors

Every onboarding cycle creates similar challenges.

# Key Insight

Most existing solutions help employees find information.

Few solutions help employees understand:

* What matters
* Why it matters
* Who to learn from
* What to learn next

Understanding is the gap Knovara aims to address.

---

# Hypothesis

Organizations will pay for a product that:

* Reduces onboarding time
* Reduces interruptions to senior engineers
* Improves knowledge transfer
* Accelerates engineer productivity

If Knovara can significantly reduce time-to-productivity, it creates measurable business value.
